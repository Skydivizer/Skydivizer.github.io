    About Me

    I was born in Germany, and at 8 years old, I made the involuntary decision to move to the Netherlands for a change of scenery. After completing a bachelor’s in Computing Science and a master’s in Logic at the University of Amsterdam, I ventured further afield to pursue a PhD in Mathematics in Las Cruces, New Mexico. My research focuses on Priestley duality and pointfree topology, and I’m passionate about exploring the interactions between different branches of mathematics. I plan to continue in academia as a postdoctoral researcher after finishing my degree.

    I’ve always loved rain and cool weather, so New Mexico’s arid climate might not have been the most logical choice, but it’s been an adventure. Outside of mathematics, I enjoy unwinding with video games, bad reality TV, or sports. I can never say no to chocolate-covered peanuts or a good beer.
